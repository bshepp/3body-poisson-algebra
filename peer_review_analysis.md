## Overall Assessment

The paper presents a creative algebraic approach to non-integrability of the three-body problem by studying the Poisson algebra generated by pairwise Hamiltonians. The polynomial representation trick (introducing u_ij = 1/r_ij) is clever and enables computations that would otherwise be intractable. The dimension sequence 3, 6, 17, 116 through level 3 appears solid. However, the paper has several issues ranging from logical gaps to presentation concerns that should be addressed before submission.

**2026-02-15 UPDATE:** A deep adversarial analysis was conducted using three independent AI agents (literature search, adversarial attack on non-integrability claim, adversarial attack on mass invariance claim). Key findings:

1. **No theorem exists** connecting Poisson algebra growth to non-integrability (searched Morales-Ramis, Ziglin, Mishchenko-Fomenko, Trofimov, Bolsinov, Nekhoroshev, AKS, GK dimension, superintegrable systems). The gap is fundamental, not just a missing citation.
2. **The non-integrability certificate claim is DEAD:** The Calogero-Moser system (completely integrable, 1/r^2 potential) produces the IDENTICAL dimension sequence 3, 6, 17, 116 through level 3. The bracket algebra growth does NOT distinguish integrability from non-integrability.
3. **The mass invariance claim survived 7 angles of adversarial attack** with no counterexample found, but the proof mechanism ("multiplicative coefficients") is demonstrably false and "for all positive masses" should be "for generic positive masses."
4. **Required reframing:** The dimension sequence is a universal algebraic invariant of the three-body pairwise decomposition, independent of the specific potential. Remove all non-integrability claims. The paper should present this as a structural result about Hamiltonian decompositions.

## Major Issues

### 1. The non-integrability argument (Section 4.1) has a logical gap

The paper claims: "If the system were Liouville-integrable with analytic first integrals F_1, ..., F_n in involution, the Poisson algebra generated by the H_ij would be constrained to a finite-dimensional quotient modulo the ideal generated by {F_k, ·}."

This is not a standard result and is not proved or cited. In an integrable system on R^12 with 6 integrals in involution, the space of smooth functions is still infinite-dimensional. The claim that the *specific* algebra generated by the H_ij under brackets would be constrained to a finite-dimensional quotient requires proof.

Even for a completely integrable system, if you take Hamiltonians that are "generic" enough, their iterated Poisson brackets could still span an infinite-dimensional space of functions — those functions would just happen to be expressible in terms of the integrals, but not in a finite-dimensional Lie algebra sense.

**2026-02-15 UPDATE:** An exhaustive literature search confirms no such theorem exists. The gap is fundamental: Liouville integrability constrains the dynamics (quasi-periodic motion on tori), not the algebraic structure of arbitrary Poisson subalgebras of C^∞(M). In action-angle coordinates, iterated brackets of generic analytic functions can span infinite-dimensional spaces even in integrable systems. The closest results (Nekhoroshev non-commutative integrability, superintegrable polynomial algebras) go in the wrong direction or apply to different settings. See `adversarial_analysis.md` Parts I-II for the full inventory of 10 frameworks searched and the detailed counterexample analysis.

**Recommendation:** Reframe the paper. Remove the "certificate of non-integrability" claim. Present the dimension sequence and mass invariance as the main results. Frame non-integrability as context: "Our algebraic invariant complements existing Morales-Ramis non-integrability proofs." Optionally state an explicit conjecture about bracket-algebra growth rates distinguishing integrable from non-integrable systems, contingent on the Calogero-Moser comparison experiment.

### 2. The mass invariance proof sketch (Theorem 2) is incomplete

The Zariski density argument is correctly applied: the generic rank of a rational matrix is constant on a Zariski-open dense set, and can only drop on a proper subvariety. Four evaluation points all giving rank 116 is strong evidence the generic rank is 116.

However, the key claim — "The masses appear only as multiplicative coefficients on terms, never determining which monomials are present" (line 295) — is exactly what needs to be proved. The kinetic energy terms T_i = p_i^2/(2m_i) have masses in the denominator. When computing iterated brackets, you get rational functions of masses multiplying various monomials. The claim that no mass-dependent cancellation can eliminate a monomial entirely requires showing that each monomial's coefficient (as a rational function of m_1, m_2, m_3) is not identically zero.

The argument is also asymmetric: the paper correctly notes that equal masses (maximal S_3 symmetry) would be the prime candidate for extra dependencies, and the fact that equal masses give 116 confirms the generic rank is 116. But the paper then claims the dimension is constant *for all* positive masses — the Zariski argument actually allows rank drops at special mass values (just not at generic ones). The paper doesn't clearly distinguish between "generically constant" and "everywhere constant."

**2026-02-15 UPDATE:** An adversarial agent attacked this claim from 7 angles and could not construct any counterexample. Key findings: (a) the "multiplicative coefficients" claim on line 294-295 is demonstrably false — masses appear in sums of rational functions, not just as multiplicative prefactors; the *correct* structural argument is that u_ij monomial patterns are mass-independent and different patterns cannot cancel regardless of mass values; (b) the test particle limit (m_3→0) does NOT provide a counterexample because the approach involves poles, not rank drops; (c) the equal-mass case IS correctly the worst case. See `adversarial_analysis.md` Part III for full details.

**Recommendation:** (1) Replace "for all positive masses" with "for generic positive masses (rank 116 confirmed at all tested configurations)" in the theorem statement. (2) Replace the "multiplicative coefficients" argument with the correct u_ij monomial pattern argument. (3) For a full proof: compute relevant maximal minors symbolically as polynomials in (m_1, m_2, m_3) and verify they have no positive real roots (Coq path).

### 3. The level-4 result is weaker than presented

From the session log, the computed dimension *increases with sample count*: 1,696 (3k points) → 2,236 (5k) → 2,725 (8k), with no SVD gap at any count. This means:

- The true dimension of level 4 is unknown — could be anywhere from 2,725 to ~11,523
- Float64 precision is insufficient to resolve all independence relationships
- The "lower bound" d(4) >= 2,725 is valid but the paper understates the uncertainty

The paper says "No SVD gap is detected, suggesting the true dimension is much larger" — this is honest. But it then uses the >= 2,725 figure to compute growth ratios and fit exponentials, which implicitly treats it as meaningful when it's a numerical artifact of 8,000 sample points at float64 precision.

**Recommendation:** Be explicit that d(4) is numerically unresolved. Consider running with mpmath at 50-digit precision on a subset of brackets. Report the sample-count dependence directly (the session_log table is more informative than what's in the paper).

## Moderate Issues

### 5. The polynomial representation's validity needs explicit justification

The introduction of u_ij as independent variables, symbolic computation in the extended ring, then evaluation at constrained points (where u_ij = 1/r_ij) is valid but should be justified explicitly:

- State that the Poisson bracket is computed only over the canonical variables (x_i, y_i, p_{x_i}, p_{y_i}), not the u_ij
- Clarify that the chain rule in Section 2.1 correctly accounts for u_ij dependence on coordinates
- Note that the rank at constrained evaluation points gives the rank in the actual function space, not the (larger) extended polynomial ring

Without this, a reader might worry about spurious independence from the extended variable space.

### 6. SVD gap criterion needs concrete data

The threshold sigma_k / sigma_{k+1} > 10^4 is stated but actual gap ratios for levels 0-3 are not reported in the paper. From the session log, the level-3 gap was 3.6 × 10^6 and level-2 was 3.2 × 10^13 — both definitive. These numbers should appear in the paper. Also discuss the condition number of the evaluation matrix, as it affects confidence in the level-4 result.

### 7. Tidal competition interpretation mixes notation

The formula for K_1 on lines 345-349 uses q-dot notation (Lagrangian velocities) which is not defined and mixes Lagrangian with Hamiltonian formalism. The cleaner form from the seed document — K_1 = (1/m_1)[(F_13 - F_12) · p_1] — is more precise and consistent with the paper's Hamiltonian framework.

### 8. The stability atlas section (Future Direction item 6) is disproportionate but has merit

Lines 438-503 (~65 lines) read as a separate research proposal rather than a future direction. Should be cut to 1-2 paragraphs or moved to supplementary material.

More importantly, the claim that boundaries between low-rank and high-rank regions would "trace the edges of KAM tori" (line 469) is too precise. The defensible claim is that regions of low local SVD rank are *candidate regions where KAM tori may exist* — the algebraic structure identifies where near-integrability is possible, not the precise boundaries of quasi-periodic motion. This is a statement about necessary conditions, not sufficient ones. Suggested rewording: low-rank regions "may contain KAM tori" rather than their boundaries "trace KAM tori edges."

The core idea — local rank drops near equilibria correlating with known stability regions — is computationally testable and worth pursuing. If rank drops correlate with the Routh stability criterion or Hill stability boundaries, that's publishable without invoking KAM theory at all.

Key practical concern: distinguishing genuine local algebraic dependence from numerical artifacts of sampling in a small ball. Functions that differ by O(epsilon^2) terms will look dependent at scale epsilon even if globally independent. Methodology would need careful epsilon-dependence analysis and comparison against null models (rank at random configurations at the same epsilon).

## Minor Issues

### 9. Double-counting of kinetic energy
Line 115: H = H_12 + H_13 + H_23 - T_1 - T_2 - T_3 — add a note explaining that each body participates in two pairs, so each T_i appears in two H_ij.

### 10. The Theta(n^2) conjecture is thin
Line 401: log d(n) = Theta(n^2) rests on 4 data points (5 with the unreliable level-4 bound). Consider downgrading from "Conjecture" to "Remark" or providing a fit with error bars.

### 11. Missing references
- Bruns' theorem (1887) on non-existence of algebraic integrals
- Julliard-Tosel's work on planar three-body non-integrability
- A proper reference for Gelfand-Kirillov dimension (Gelfand & Kirillov, 1966)
- Ziglin's original non-integrability results

### 12. The broken OEIS match is more interesting than the non-match
Line 393 notes the sequence doesn't appear in OEIS. But the session log reveals the *numerical* approach initially found a match to A114491 (matroid-related) that broke at level 3 when exact computation gave 116 instead of 69. This is worth mentioning — it demonstrates that the exact polynomial representation was essential and that naive numerical approaches give misleading results.

### 13. Filtration terminology
The definition on lines 121-125 is a "bracket-closure" filtration, not the lower central series or derived series. A reader from Lie algebra theory might assume one of the standard filtrations. Label it explicitly.

## What Coq Could Verify

Highest-value formal verifications, in priority order:

1. **Exact Poisson bracket computation** — Formalize the 15-variable polynomial ring, the chain rule for u_ij, and verify that {H_12, H_13} produces the claimed expression. This is the foundation.

2. **Jacobi identity** — Proved numerically (10^-14 errors). A Coq proof over exact rationals would be definitive.

3. **d(0)=3, d(1)=6, d(2)=17** — Tractable in exact arithmetic. Level 2 involves a 17 × 15 matrix over Q(m_1, m_2, m_3).

4. **Mass invariance for levels 0-2** — Compute the rank of the coefficient matrix symbolically in the masses and show the relevant minors are non-zero polynomials. This would be the most publishable verification.

5. **d(3)=116** — Harder (156 × N matrix) but the symbolic expressions exist in checkpoints/.

The key gap Coq could close is the mass invariance: showing that the 40 dependencies at level 3 arise solely from structural identities (Jacobi + chain rule), not mass-dependent cancellations. This would upgrade Theorem 2 from a proof sketch to a theorem.

## Summary

The core idea is creative and the methodology is sound through level 3. The main risks are:

1. The non-integrability argument needs to be made rigorous or flagged as heuristic
2. The mass invariance proof needs completion
3. The level-4 result needs more honest uncertainty quantification

The dimension sequence 3, 6, 17, 116 and its mass invariance are genuinely novel and worth publishing even without the stronger non-integrability claim. The paper should tighten claims to match evidence — which is still strong.
