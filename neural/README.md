# Neural Network Poisson Bracket Algebra

Computes the Lie algebra generated by pairwise interaction Hamiltonians of a
minimal neural network's training dynamics under the Poisson bracket.

## Setup

A 3-layer linear network `f(x) = w₃·w₂·w₁·x` trained with SGD+momentum is a
Hamiltonian system on a 6-dimensional phase space:

- **Coordinates:** weights `(w₁, w₂, w₃)`
- **Momenta:** velocity buffer `(v₁, v₂, v₃)`
- **Kinetic energy:** `T_i = v_i² / 2`
- **Pairwise potential:** gradient-product coupling `V_ij = (∂L/∂w_i)(∂L/∂w_j) / 2`

where `L = (w₃·w₂·w₁ - 1)² / 2` is the MSE loss (x=1, target=1).

## Main Result

| Algebra | Dimension sequence | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| **Neural (gradient-product)** | **[3, 6, 17, 119]** | Exact over ℚ |
| Gravitational (1/r) | [3, 6, 17, 116] | Reference |
| Quantum ℏ-deformed | [3, 6, 17, 117] | 1 extra generator |

The neural algebra has **3 extra generators** at level 3 compared to the
gravitational universal sequence, all in the standard (2D) representation of S₃:

| Index | Bracket | Terms | Momentum degrees | S₃ rep |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------------|--------|
| 125 | `{{K₂,H₂₃},{K₃,H₂₃}}` | 502 | v¹/v³ (odd) | standard |
| 128 | `{{K₂,K₃},H₂₃}` | 258 | v⁰/v² (even) | standard |
| 152 | `{{K₃,H₂₃},H₂₃}` | 73 | v¹/v³ (odd) | standard |

All prominently involve H₂₃ at the outermost bracket level. The gradient-product
potential has polynomial degree 10 (vs degree 1 for gravitational −u_ij),
allowing richer bracket structure at level 3.
