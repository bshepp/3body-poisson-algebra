ANALYSIS
==================================================

1. HARMONIC POTENTIAL: FRAMEWORK VALIDATED
   The r² potential is integrable (it's a system of coupled
   harmonic oscillators). The Poisson algebra stabilises at
   dim = 15 with ZERO new generators at level 4. This confirms
   that the framework correctly detects integrability: integrable
   systems produce finite-dimensional algebras.

2. CALOGERO-MOSER: SURPRISING RESULT
   The 1/r² potential gives the IDENTICAL dimension sequence
   [3, 6, 17, 116] as the 1/r (Newton) potential.

   Key insight: Calogero-Moser integrability holds for 1D
   particles on a line (any N), not for particles in the PLANE.
   The planar three-body problem with 1/r² potential is NOT
   known to be integrable for scalar particles without spin
   degrees of freedom. The super-exponential growth we observe
   is consistent with this non-integrability.

   The identical dimension sequence for 1/r and 1/r² suggests
   that the growth depends on the SINGULARITY TYPE of the
   potential (inverse-distance), not the specific power.

3. DICHOTOMY: SINGULAR vs REGULAR POTENTIALS
   The framework distinguishes sharply between:
   - Singular potentials (1/r, 1/r²): infinite-dim algebra,
     identical dimension sequence [3, 6, 17, 116, ...]
   - Regular potentials (r²): finite-dim algebra, dim = 15

   This is consistent with the Morales-Ramis framework, where
   the singularity structure of the potential determines the
   differential Galois group and hence integrability properties.

4. THE HARMONIC DIMENSION: d = 15
   For the planar 3-body harmonic oscillator, the algebra
   stabilises at dim 15.  The phase space has dim 12, so
   15 > 12 indicates the algebra includes functions that are
   not independent on each energy shell (Casimirs reduce the
   effective dimension).  This should be computable analytically
   from the known symmetry algebra of coupled oscillators.
