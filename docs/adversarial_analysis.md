## Part I: Literature Search — Does the Theorem Exist?

### Executive Summary

**No clean, citable theorem exists** connecting infinite-dimensional growth of a Poisson subalgebra generated by Hamiltonians to non-integrability of a Hamiltonian system. This is not a gap in our search — it is a gap in the literature, and there are fundamental reasons why.

### What Was Searched

Extensive search across: Morales-Ramis, Ziglin, Mishchenko-Fomenko, Trofimov-Fomenko, Bolsinov, Nekhoroshev, Adler-Kostant-Symes, Gelfand-Kirillov dimension, superintegrable systems, non-commutative integrability, bi-Hamiltonian systems, Poisson-Lie group theory. Both web searches and knowledge-base analysis.

### Why No Clean Theorem Likely Exists

The fundamental reason is structural:

> **Liouville integrability is a statement about the existence of n functions in involution. It constrains the *dynamics* (motion is quasi-periodic on tori), not the *algebraic structure of arbitrary subalgebras of C^∞(M)*.**

In action-angle coordinates (I_k, θ_k), any analytic function has a Fourier expansion in the θ variables. The Poisson bracket {f,g} in action-angle coordinates can introduce new Fourier modes:

{f, g} = Σ_k (∂f/∂I_k · ∂g/∂θ_k − ∂f/∂θ_k · ∂g/∂I_k)

Iterated brackets of generic analytic functions can produce functions with arbitrarily high Fourier modes, spanning an infinite-dimensional space — even though the dynamics is completely integrable. The brackets don't automatically truncate to a finite-dimensional algebra.

### The Closest Results (Partial)

1. **Nekhoroshev contrapositive (almost applies):** If the H_ij were conserved quantities, then their generating an infinite-dimensional algebra would preclude non-commutative integrability. But H_ij are NOT conserved — they include the total H as a linear combination, but individual H_ij are not integrals.

2. **Superintegrable systems:** For superintegrable systems, integrals form finite-dimensional polynomial Poisson algebras. This is the right direction, but only applies to superintegrability (more integrals than degrees of freedom), not Liouville integrability.

3. **Empirical pattern (AKS/Toda/Calogero-Moser):** In every known construction of an integrable multi-body system via algebraic methods, the relevant Poisson algebra is finite-dimensional or has polynomial growth. No integrable system is known to have super-exponential bracket-algebra growth. This is empirical evidence, not a theorem.

### Possible Restricted Theorem (Sketch, Unproved)

> **Possible theorem:** If the planar three-body system were Liouville-integrable with first integrals that are *algebraic functions of the phase space variables* (elements of the ring generated by the H_ij and their iterated brackets), then d(n) would be bounded by a polynomial in n.

This requires the integrals to live *inside* the generated algebra — a strong assumption but natural if one believes integrals should be "constructible" from the Hamiltonians.

## Part II: Adversarial Attack on the Non-Integrability Certificate

### Attack 1 — FATAL: The Central Claim Is Unjustified

The paper claims: "If the system were Liouville-integrable...the Poisson algebra generated by the H_ij would be constrained to a finite-dimensional quotient."

This is neither proved, cited, nor true in general.

**Counterexample logic:** The Calogero-Moser 3-body system (rational, 1/r² potential) is completely Liouville-integrable (Moser, 1975). Define the exact same pairwise Hamiltonians:

- H_12^CM = p_1²/2 + p_2²/2 + g²/(q_1 − q_2)²
- H_13^CM = p_1²/2 + p_3²/2 + g²/(q_1 − q_3)²
- H_23^CM = p_2²/2 + p_3²/2 + g²/(q_2 − q_3)²

The bracket {H_12^CM, H_13^CM} has the same "tidal competition" structure. Iterated brackets produce increasingly complex rational functions of inter-particle distances. The growth of d(k) for this integrable system is the critical unknown.

**If Calogero-Moser also grows super-exponentially → the certificate claim is dead.**
**If Calogero-Moser closes or grows polynomially → the paper's intuition is empirically vindicated.**

This is the single most important computation for the paper. It is mentioned as Future Direction 2 but has not been done.

### Attack 2 — SERIOUS: Linear vs Functional Independence

The paper measures d(k) via SVD rank of evaluation matrices. This detects *linear* independence (no constant-coefficient linear combination is identically zero), NOT functional independence (no smooth relation F(f_1,...,f_k) = 0 exists).

On a 12-dimensional phase space, at most 12 functions can be functionally independent. d(3) = 116 means 116 linearly independent functions satisfying at least 104 nonlinear functional relations.

The super-exponential growth of d(k) may reflect the *algebraic complexity of the expressions* (that 1/r generates complicated rational functions under differentiation) rather than any property of the dynamics. The 1/r² potential would likely also generate algebraically complex expressions.

**The paper should clarify:** d(k) measures the dimension of a linear span of functions, which is an algebraic invariant of the Hamiltonian decomposition, not directly a measure of dynamical complexity.

### Attack 3 — FATAL: No Mechanism Linking Integrability to Finite Bracket Closure

The paper has the Mishchenko-Fomenko implication backwards:

- Mishchenko-Fomenko: finite-dim Lie algebra of integrals → integrability (sufficient condition)
- Paper claims: integrability → finite-dim bracket closure of sub-Hamiltonians (no theorem supports this)

The H_ij are not integrals of motion. Their mutual brackets are not constrained by the existence of integrals commuting with their sum H.

### Attack 4 — MODERATE: Redundancy with Known Results

The three-body problem's non-integrability is already established by:
- Bruns (1887): no additional algebraic integrals
- Poincare (1890): no additional analytic integrals
- Tsygvintsev (2001-2007): Morales-Ramis analysis
- Maciejewski & Przybylska (2011): differential Galois theory

Without an independent logical chain from algebra growth to non-integrability, the "certificate" is at best *consistent with* known results, not an independent proof.

### Attack 5 — MODERATE: Numerical Methodology

- Level 4 is unresolved (d increases with sample count: 1,696 → 2,236 → 2,725, no SVD gap)
- SVD tests linear independence, not functional independence (see Attack 2)
- The polynomial representation makes symbolic expressions more complex than actual phase-space functions, potentially inflating apparent algebraic growth

## Part III: Adversarial Attack on Mass Invariance (Theorem 2)

### Executive Summary

After systematic attack from seven angles, **the conclusion is almost certainly correct but the proof as written has identifiable flaws.** The adversarial agent could not construct any counterexample despite trying every angle available.

### Attack 1 — Zariski Argument: Genuine Logical Gap

The proof establishes the *generic* rank is 116. It does NOT establish the rank is 116 *for all positive masses*.

The Zariski argument explicitly allows rank drops on a proper algebraic subvariety. The theorem statement says "for all positive masses" but the proof only delivers "for generic positive masses."

**The paper internally contradicts itself:** Line 430 says "A complete proof would require showing that no proper algebraic subvariety of mass space admits extra dependencies," while the theorem statement on line 252 claims universality.

**Fix:** Replace "for all positive masses" with "for generic positive masses (rank 116 confirmed at all tested configurations including equal masses and Tsygvintsev exceptional cases)."

### Attack 2 — "Multiplicative Coefficients" Claim: FALSE AS STATED

Line 294-295 claims: "The masses appear only as multiplicative coefficients on terms, never determining which monomials are present."

This is demonstrably false. When computing {H_12, H_13}:

```
dH_12/dx_1 = G·m_1·m_2·(x_1−x_2)·u_12³
dH_13/dp_{x1} = p_{x1}/m_1
```

The contribution (dH_12/dx_1)(dH_13/dp_{x1}) = G·m_2·(x_1−x_2)·u_12³·p_{x1} — note m_1 cancelled.

At higher bracket levels, a single monomial in phase-space variables can receive contributions from multiple terms, each with a different rational function of masses as coefficient. The total coefficient is a *sum* of rational functions, which could in principle vanish at specific mass values. This is NOT "multiplicative."

**The correct structural argument** (which the paper should use instead): the monomial structure in the u_ij variables is mass-independent. Different u_ij exponent patterns cannot cancel against each other regardless of mass values. The masses determine coefficients *within* each u_ij pattern but not *which patterns appear*.

### Attack 3 — Test Particle Limit (m_3 → 0): FAILS

As m_3 → 0, the kinetic term p_3²/(2m_3) diverges. The entries of the coefficient matrix have *poles* at m_3 = 0, not zeros. The matrix isn't even well-defined at m_3 = 0.

After clearing denominators by multiplying by powers of m_1·m_2·m_3, the polynomial entries at m_3 = 0 include p_3²/2 terms that are independent of m_3, so the cleared-denominator matrix still has nontrivial rank.

**This attack fails because m_3 = 0 is outside the domain ("all positive masses") and the approach involves poles, not rank drops.**

### Attack 4 — Equal Masses: FAILS

Equal masses (maximal S_3 symmetry) are the worst case for extra dependencies. d(3) = 116 at equal masses means all 116 generators are genuinely independent even with maximal symmetry. S_3 creates maps between generators but doesn't make them coincide as functions on phase space.

### Attack 5 — Well-Definedness: NO ISSUE

For strictly positive masses, all denominators (products of powers of m_i) are positive. No poles in the domain.

### Attack 6 — Explicit Coefficient Tracing: NO COUNTEREXAMPLE

At levels 0-1, mass-dependent cancellation is provably impossible (the K_i involve structurally distinct u_ij monomials with single-mass-variable coefficients).

At levels 2-3, the structural pattern persists but formalization requires explicit computation over all 156 generators.

### Attack 7 — Untested Mass Ratios

Hierarchical masses (m_1 >> m_2 >> m_3), algebraic relations (m_1 = m_2 + m_3), etc. — none could be shown to cause rank drops. The coefficient magnitudes change but the algebraic structure (which monomials are present) does not.

### Verdict on Mass Invariance

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Generic rank = 116 | PROVED (Zariski + 4 evaluations) |
| Rank = 116 for all positive masses | VERY STRONG CONJECTURE, not proved |
| "Multiplicative coefficients" proof mechanism | FALSE as stated (should use u_ij monomial pattern argument) |
| Any counterexample found | NO, after 7 angles of attack |
| Recommended fix | Downgrade theorem statement; fix proof mechanism |

## Part V: The Critical Experiment — Calogero-Moser Comparison

Both adversarial agents independently identified this as **the single most important computation for the paper**.

### Setup

The rational Calogero-Moser 3-body system (PLANAR, matching the paper's setup):

H_CM = Σ p_i²/(2m_i) + g²/r_12² + g²/r_13² + g²/r_23²

This system is **completely Liouville-integrable** (Moser, 1975). Integrals: I_1 = Σp_i, I_2 = H_CM, I_3 = Tr(L³) where L is the Lax matrix.

Define pairwise Hamiltonians:
- H_12^CM = p_1²/(2m_1) + p_2²/(2m_2) + g²/r_12²
- H_13^CM = p_1²/(2m_1) + p_3²/(2m_3) + g²/r_13²
- H_23^CM = p_2²/(2m_2) + p_3²/(2m_3) + g²/r_23²

Compute d_CM(0), d_CM(1), d_CM(2), d_CM(3) using the same methodology.

### RESULTS (Computed 2026-02-14/15)

#### Through Level 3: PERFECT MATCH (Symbolic/Exact)

|-------|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0 | 3 | 3 | YES |
| 1 | 6 | 6 | YES |
| 2 | 17 | 17 | YES |
| 3 (symbolic, exact) | **116** | **116** | **YES** |

Both systems give IDENTICAL dimensions through level 3. The SVD gap is DEFINITIVE for both:
- Gravitational level 3: gap ratio ~10^13

#### Numerical Results Were MISLEADING

| Level | Gravitational (numerical) | CM (numerical) | Match? |

The numerical finite-difference method UNDERCOUNTED both systems, but undercounted CM MORE due to worse numerical conditioning (CM expressions involve higher powers of u_ij, creating larger dynamic range). The apparent divergence was entirely an artifact of numerical precision. This is a cautionary tale about trusting numerical rank determination for distinguishing systems.

#### Interpretation

The dimension sequence 3, 6, 17, 116 is a property of the **pairwise Hamiltonian decomposition structure** (3 bodies, 2D, kinetic + pairwise potential), NOT a signature of non-integrability. Both the non-integrable gravitational system (-1/r potential) and the completely integrable Calogero-Moser system (1/r^2 potential) produce the IDENTICAL dimension sequence.

The only observed difference is in the SVD gap magnitude: gravitational gaps are ~10^13 while CM gaps are ~10^5 at level 3. This difference in conditioning may or may not be dynamically meaningful — it could simply reflect the different algebraic complexity of the two potentials (u vs u^2 in the polynomial representation).
